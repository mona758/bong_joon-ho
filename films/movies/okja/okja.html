Okja follows the story of Mija, a young girl who has raised a genetically modified super pig named Okja in the mountains of South Korea. When the Mirando Corporation, led by the eccentric Lucy Mirando, comes to reclaim Okja as part of a global contest to find the "best" super pig, Mija must embark on a dangerous mission to rescue her beloved companion. The film weaves together a touching bond between Mija and Okja with a satirical critique of corporate greed, the meat industry, and animal exploitation. Directed by Bong Joon-ho, Okja blends adventure, humor, and social commentary to explore the lengths one will go to for love, while also confronting the consequences of human greed and industrial practices.

Trivia & Fun Fact

A Unique Production Style

Okja was one of the first major films to be produced by Netflix and was released simultaneously in theaters and on the streaming platform. This approach was groundbreaking for the film industry at the time.

CGI and Practical Effects

While Okja is a CGI-animated character, much of the interaction with the super pig was done using practical effects, including a life-sized animatronic version of Okja. The use of both CGI and practical effects made Okja feel more tangible and relatable.

Filming Locations

Although set in various locations across the world, including the United States and South Korea, the film was largely shot in South Korea. The scenes in the mountains were filmed in the beautiful landscapes of the countryâ€™s northern regions.

The Film's Environmental Message

Okja critiques the factory farming industry and issues of corporate greed, touching on themes of animal rights and the ethics of genetically modified organisms (GMOs). Bong Joon-ho worked closely with animal rights groups to ensure the film conveyed these messages authentically.
