Mother is a gripping, psychological thriller about a devoted single mother who takes matters into her own hands when her son, Do-joon, is accused of a brutal murder he may not have committed. Despite his intellectual disability, Do-joon is framed for the crime, and the film follows his mother's relentless search for the truth. As she unravels the mystery, she faces difficult moral choices, and the boundaries between justice and vengeance blur. Directed by Bong Joon-ho, Mother explores themes of sacrifice, maternal love, and the lengths to which a mother will go to protect her child. The film delivers a powerful emotional punch and builds an intricate, suspenseful narrative, while delving into the complexities of familial bonds and societal expectations. The mother’s fight to prove her son’s innocence reveals disturbing secrets about their small community and forces viewers to question the nature of justice.

Trivia & Fun Fact

Realism of the Role

Kim Hye-ja, who played the mother, underwent extensive preparation for her role, even learning how to carry out some of the character's actions, like using acupuncture needles. She also sought advice from real mothers in difficult situations to better understand her role.

First Collaboration

Mother marked the first collaboration between Bong Joon-ho and cinematographer Hong Kyung-pyo, who would go on to work together again in Snowpiercer (2013).

The Importance of the Village Setting

The rural village setting in the film was an essential part of the storytelling. The isolation of the town symbolizes the emotional and physical distance between the mother and the rest of society.

Won Bin’s Break from Acting

Mother was one of the last films Won Bin did before taking a break from acting for several years. His performance in this movie was highly acclaimed and has remained one of his most iconic roles.

Awards Recognition

Kim Hye-ja's performance in Mother earned her widespread recognition, including a Best Actress nomination at the 2010 Asian Film Awards and the Best Actress award at the 2009 San Sebastián International Film Festival.

Cast & Crew

Kim Hye-ja

Mother (Hye-ja)

A veteran South Korean actress, Kim Hye-ja is renowned for her versatility in both film and television. She is best known for her role in Mother, where she portrays a fiercely protective mother who will stop at nothing to protect her son. Kim won numerous awards for her performance, showcasing her deep emotional range. Her career spans decades, and she has been recognized as one of Korea’s most respected actresses.

Actress

Won Bin

Do-joon

A former model turned actor, Won Bin gained fame for his roles in Autumn in My Heart and The Man From Nowhere. In Mother, he plays Do-joon, a mentally challenged man wrongly accused of a crime, whose relationship with his mother is central to the plot. Known for his good looks and stoic roles, Won Bin brings a complex emotional depth to his character

Actor

Yoon Je-moon

Detective Lee

Known for his strong supporting roles, Yoon Je-moon plays Detective Lee, a local investigator who becomes involved in the case surrounding Do-joon. His performance is grounded and understated, providing a stark contrast to the intense emotional performances of the leads.

actor

Jin Goo

The Lawyer

Jin Goo is a well-respected actor in South Korea, known for his roles in The Good, The Bad, The Weird and A Dirty Carnival. In Mother, he plays a lawyer who is tasked with defending Do-joon, bringing a certain legal coldness to the drama.

Park No-shik

The Pharmacist

A character actor with a long career in film, Park No-shik plays a seemingly minor character who has important information for the investigation, adding to the tense atmosphere of the film.
