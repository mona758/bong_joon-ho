Snowpiercer (2013) is a dystopian science fiction thriller set in a future where Earth has become an uninhabitable frozen wasteland after a failed climate experiment. The only survivors live on a perpetually moving train, the Snowpiercer, which endlessly circles the globe. Inside, society is strictly divided by class, with the wealthy and privileged occupying the front cars while the oppressed and impoverished are confined to the back. Led by Curtis Everett, the tail-end passengers launch a desperate rebellion to reach the engine and confront the trainâ€™s creator, Mr. Wilford. The journey uncovers secrets about the train, the world outside, and the true cost of survival.

Trivia & Fun Fact

Inspired by a Graphic Novel

Snowpiercer is based on the French graphic novel Le Transperceneige by Jacques Lob and Jean-Marc Rochette. Bong Joon-ho was captivated by it and envisioned a film adaptation.

Bongâ€™s Unique Cast Choice

Bong Joon-ho specifically wanted Song Kang-ho for the role of Namgoong Minsoo and adjusted the script to fit him, showcasing the collaborationâ€™s significance.

Filmed in a Real Train Set

To capture the confined space of the train, a massive train set was built with actual compartments, enhancing the claustrophobic, gritty atmosphere.

Hidden Messages in Design

Minister Mason, portrayed by Tilda Swinton, wore odd and outdated fashion as Bong wanted her to look like a figure of absurd authority, embodying the satire of bureaucratic corruption.

A 30-Year Journey

The Snowpiercer train is designed to circle the globe annually, with each lap taking exactly one year, symbolizing the unchanging cycle of class oppression.

Symbolic Fish Scene

The scene where guards use fish blood for a ritual before fighting was Bongâ€™s idea to show the brutality and ritualistic loyalty within the train's elite.

International Success

Snowpiercer became the highest-grossing Korean film in international markets at the time, breaking new ground for Korean cinema abroad.

Climate Change Commentary

The movie subtly critiques environmental and political issues, highlighting humanityâ€™s short-sighted actions leading to environmental collapse and extreme social hierarchies.

Cast & Crew

Song Kang-ho

Namgoong Minsoo

Song Kang-ho is a celebrated South Korean actor, known for his work in films such as Memories of Murder (2003), The Host (2006), and Parasite (2019). Often cast in leading roles that explore social and political issues, Song is regarded as one of Korea's most versatile actors. In Snowpiercer, he plays Namgoong Minsoo, a former prisoner and security expert who helps the rebels navigate the train's security systems.

Curtis Everett

Chris Evans is an American actor known for his portrayal of Captain America in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). He began his career with roles in television and films like Not Another Teen Movie (2001) before rising to fame with Captain America: The First Avenger (2011). Evans has shown a range of acting ability in various genres, including action, drama, and sci-fi. His role as Curtis Everett in Snowpiercer shows his talent for complex, gritty characters who fight for survival in dystopian worlds.

Tilda Swinton is a British actress known for her distinctive and often eccentric roles. She has won numerous awards for her performances in films such as Michael Clayton (2007) and We Need to Talk About Kevin (2011). Swinton's portrayal of Minister Mason in Snowpiercer stands out for her over-the-top character, an embodiment of the oppressive, class-dividing authority on the train. Her style often blends the avant-garde with mainstream cinema, making her one of the most unique talents in the industry.
