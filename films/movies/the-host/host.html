Trivia & Fun Fact

Record-Breaking Popularity

The Host became South Korea's highest-grossing film at the time of its release in 2006, drawing over 13 million viewers in theaters.

Inspired by Real Events

The storyline about toxic waste dumping into the Han River is based on an actual 2000 incident when a U.S. military official authorized formaldehyde disposal into the river, causing public outrage in South Korea.

Real-Life Influences

Director Bong Joon-ho drew inspiration from monster films like Jaws and Godzilla but infused The Host with Korean cultural elements and political themes, making it uniquely resonant.

One-Year Editing Process

Creating the creature's special effects took over a year, with the animation handled by renowned studios, including the team behind The Lord of the Rings.

Multiple Genres

The Host blends horror, sci-fi, comedy, and family drama. Bong Joon-hoâ€™s genre-bending style in this film set the tone for his future works.
