Synopsis Parasite tells the story of two families—the impoverished Kim family and the wealthy Park family—whose lives become entwined in unexpected ways. Through a mix of dark humor and suspense, the film explores themes of class disparity, survival, and the hidden connections between people. As the Kims integrate themselves into the Parks' lives, tensions build, leading to unforeseen consequences.

Trivia & Fun Fact

Real House or Set?

The wealthy Park family’s house wasn’t a real home. It was an elaborate set built specifically for the film, designed to match Bong Joon-ho's vision. Every detail, from the lighting to the layout, was meticulously planned.

Historical Firsts

Parasite made history by becoming the first South Korean film to win the Palme d'Or at the Cannes Film Festival and the first non-English language film to win the Academy Award for Best Picture.

Symbolic Use of Stairs

Bong Joon-ho used the architecture and elevation in the film to represent the social class divide, with characters constantly moving up or down stairs to symbolize their social status.

Improvised Scenes

Some of the funniest scenes were improvised by the cast, including the part where Ki-woo teaches his sister Ki-jung how to forge documents, showing their quick-witted approach to scamming.

Inspiration for the Story

Bong Joon-ho was inspired by his own life experiences and the stark contrast he observed between social classes in South Korea. The story was also partly inspired by a real-life friend who had a job as a tutor for a wealthy family.

Cast & Crew

Han Jin-won

Han Jin-won is a South Korean screenwriter and director best known for his work on Parasite, for which he won the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay alongside Bong Joon-ho. He also collaborated with Bong on the film Okja and has written for various other notable projects. Han’s work often explores themes of social inequality, class, and human nature, similar to Bong's directorial style.
